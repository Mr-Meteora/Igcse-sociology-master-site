0495 exam map

What the exam is actually testing

Two papers. Both 80 marks. Both worth 50%. Paper 1 is compulsory-heavy. Paper 2 makes you choose two out of Family, Education, and Crime.

Paper 1 - 2 hours
  • Research Methods is compulsory.
  • Then choose Identity or Inequality.
  • Expect data response and structured writing.
Paper 2 - 1 hour 45 minutes
  • Choose two: Family, Education, Crime.
  • Each chosen question is worth 40 marks.
  • Parts e and f need proper arguments, not vibes.

exam checklist

Before you sit the paper

  • Can I define key terms in one clean sentence?
  • Can I explain both sides of a debate?
  • Can I use named sociologists without randomly sprinkling them in?
  • Can I add examples that are short and actually relevant?
  • Can I evaluate strengths and limitations?
definition bank

Definition bank starter

  • Validity: how accurately findings reflect real life.
  • Reliability: whether research can be repeated under similar conditions.
  • Socialisation: how people learn norms, values, and behaviour.
  • Deviance: behaviour that breaks social norms.
sociologist flashcards

Name-drop responsibly

Examiners like sociologists when they actually fit the point. Do not throw Durkheim into the paragraph like seasoning and hope for the best.

DurkheimFunctionalism, social solidarity, social facts, social control.
MarxClass conflict, capitalism, exploitation, inequality.
WeberClass, status, power, life chances.
OakleyGender socialisation and family roles.
BeckerLabelling, deviance, self-fulfilling prophecy.
MertonStrain theory and deviance.
past-paper strategy

Past papers early

  • Start with open-book attempts so you learn structure.
  • Then self-mark with the mark scheme open.
  • Finally do timed papers because your hand needs training too.
  • For longer answers: point, evidence, explain, evaluate, link.

notes upgrade

What my notes feedback screamed

  • Keep top exam points, not every single possible point.
  • Use comparison tables when arguments get messy.
  • Make examples short enough to remember.
  • Rewrite in your own words. Copyright jump scare avoided.

Chapter 06 / Real talk

Advice

Aka "what actually works."

Active recall

Highlighting your notes in four different colours and calling it "studying" is not studying. Test yourself. Close the book. Say it out loud.

Spaced repetition

Use spaced repetition. Review things just before you would forget them instead of cramming.

Past papers early

Do past papers early. The exam has a format, so get familiar with it before it surprises you.

Teach someone else

Teach someone else. If you can explain a concept clearly, you actually understand it. Feynman technique for the W.
