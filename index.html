0495 exam structure

What the exam is actually testing

Two papers. Both 80 marks. Both worth 50%. Paper 1 has Research Methods as a compulsory topic, then you choose one more. Paper 2 gives you three topics and you pick two.

Paper 1 — 2 hours
  • Research Methods is compulsory.
  • Then choose Identity or Inequality.
  • Expect data response and structured writing.
Paper 2 — 1 hour 45 minutes
  • Choose two: Family, Education, or Crime.
  • Each question is worth 40 marks.
  • Parts (e) and (f) need proper arguments — not vibes.

exam checklist

Before you sit the paper

  • Can I define key terms without writing an essay?
  • Can I explain both sides of a debate?
  • Can I use named sociologists where they actually fit?
  • Can I give examples that are short and relevant?
  • Can I evaluate strengths and limitations?

past-paper strategy

How to actually use past papers

  • Start open-book so you learn the structure first.
  • Then self-mark with the mark scheme open every time.
  • Finally do timed papers — your hand needs practice too.
  • For longer answers: point, evidence, explain, evaluate, link.

Paper 2 chooser

Paper 2: choose your two wisely

Family, Education, Crime. Pick the two you can actually argue — not the two that seem easiest when you're staring at the front cover.

FamilyGood if you know roles, marriage, divorce, childhood, and sociological perspectives.
EducationGood if you can handle class, gender, ethnicity, the hidden curriculum, and labelling.
CrimeGood if you know deviance, social control, official statistics, labelling, and explanations of crime.

Chapter 05 / Exam tactics

Tips

Straightforward things that stop you dropping marks you already know how to get.

the short version

Exam survival basics

Name sociologists correctly. Use PEEL for longer answers. Keep short answers precise. Self-mark everything. Watch the clock. Use sociological vocabulary.

theory marks

Name sociologists correctly

"A sociologist said…" gets you nothing. "Durkheim argued…" gets you marks. Name them, state their theory, link it to your point.

structure

PEEL paragraphs

Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link back to the question. Use this for every extended answer. It's not glamorous but it works.

quick wins

Short-answer questions

Be precise and include a brief example where it helps. One or two sentences. Don't write three paragraphs for a 2-mark question.

feedback loop

Self-marking

Mark your own past papers with the mark scheme open every time. You learn more from understanding why an answer gets marks than from getting it right.

exam stamina

Time management

Practise under timed conditions. Your handwriting slows you down more than you think. Train your hand as much as your brain.

vocabulary

Sociological terminology

Use the correct terms throughout — social stratification, socialisation, norms, values, institutions. It signals that you actually know the subject.

Chapter 06 / Real talk

Advice

Study techniques that have actual evidence behind them — and that work for Sociology specifically.

Active recall

Highlighting notes in four colours is not revising. Close the book and try to recall what you just read. Say it out loud, write it down from memory, test yourself. It feels harder — that's the point.

Spaced repetition

Review material just before you'd forget it, not the night before the exam. Anki is designed specifically for this. It's boring to set up. It works.

Past papers from day one

The exam has a specific format and specific expectations. The only way to get comfortable with both is to practise them early. Don't save past papers for the revision period.

Teach it to someone else

If you can explain a concept clearly — to a friend, your parent, your cat — you actually understand it. If you stumble, you've found exactly what needs more work. This is the Feynman technique and it's genuinely useful.
